connect@cal.

connecting students to campus resources

overview

Role: Design lead and developer

Timeframe: Ongoing

Tools: Figma, HTML/CSS, Javascript, ReactJS

purpose

Connect@Cal is a student organization that aims to connect Berkeley students to different resources on campus. It primarily solves the issue of the overabundance of resources and the overwhelming nature of trying to find communities, organizations, and opportunities.

background

I first joined Connect@Cal back in July 2020, when the organization had just begun. As the sole designer, I worked to brand and redesign the website. I also worked to develop the Resource Finder, a web app that allows students to filter and search for resources on their own.

branding

After researching logo design and looking at other Berkeley organizations for inspiration, I came up with a few logo designs. The final logo, pictured above, models two dialog boxes connected with one another to convey students connecting students to resources. I also opted for a more subtle blue to establish a trustworthy and calming brand.

resource finder

As an organization, we knew we wanted to also provide a quicker way for students to find resourcs, rather than submitting a case and waiting for Connect@Cal's case associates to find resources for them. Thus, I worked with Eric Cheng, one of C@C's tech directors, as well as a team of developers to create the Resource Finder. I primarily drove the development of the frontend, and had to learn a bit of ReactJS to build the project.
