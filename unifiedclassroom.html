unified classroom redesign.

redesigning the assignment and grading platform

overview

Role: Sole UX/UI Designer

Timeframe: 1 month (June 2020)

Tools: Adobe XD

background

Unified Classroom is a classroom management software used by grade school students to check their grades, turn in or check their upcoming and past assignments, and view announcements by both teachers and the school. This was my first UX project done the summer before my freshman year of college, and through the BuiltByGirls WAVE program, I was also able to get feedback from UX designer Sasha Sukhoruchko.

problem

As a longtime user of Powerschool’s Unified Classroom, I have always found the site to be super “clicky” and very cluttered. I have also always heard other students complain about the site as well and thus I wanted to try to tackle the issue firsthand.

research

I interviewed 11 students, ages 14-18, who use Unified Classroom (UC) on a daily basis.

Interview Questions:
  • What do you use UC primarily for? What is your goal when you log on to UC?
  • When you first log into UC, what do you notice immediately?
  • What features of UC do you use/not use?
  • What do you like/dislike about UC?
  • Do you know where to find the missing assignments you have to do?
  • Where do you turn in your assignments?
  • Can you view the calendar in a monthly view?
  • Do you use the personal reminder feature? Do you know how to use it?
  • Anything you would like UC to have?

I also conducted a usability test with 1 student outside of UPA, who had never used Unified Classroom before.
